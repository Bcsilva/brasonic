BrazSonic 2 – 2007 to 2009

From 2007, BrazSonic 2 had been changed significantly, such as the game engines, art styles, and levels. The engine change was made in order to rebuild the project to use less memory than the previous engine.

At this time, I could redo the character styles and personalities. BrazSonic, now is from Rio de Janeiro and wears a tank top and blue flip flops; PeloTails wears a different hat, boots, scarf and shirt like an old-school southern Brazilian; and Baianuckles, is still blond, but with more rustic “cangaceiro” style.

Among the Sonic and Gamedev communities from those years, I obtained some help from some people for the game graphics. Savara, aka Mugen Hunter, author of the Mugen game Freedom Fighters, and Augusto Tirabosqui, aka Shadowboy, from the Forum Portal Sonic community, were helping with sprite work for the NPCs for enemies. Their work was so good.

Now each character, albeit the Sonic/Tails/Knuckles signature moves, has different skills for dealing with enemies and some kind of puzzles across the levels.

These skills were tough to develop and took a long time to “finish”. From now, BrazSonic had an air kick attack, and could toss his flip-flops against far targets, such flying enemies and switches; PeloTails, could toss his rodeo lasso as a grapple to swing around hooks, branches, and poles in order to reach further places, despite it could also be used as a hover (which is slower and weaker); and Baianuckles, was supposed to be a “power-type” character, with more emphasis on melee capoeira-based attacks, resulting in some combo sequences against closer enemies. Also, the yellow echidna counts with a parasol, based on the “Frevo” dance, that could be used to deflect enemies projectiles and pass through strong wind passages.

A fun fact is that the Frevo dance is not from Bahia province, but from Pernambuco. My fail… but it could be a part of learned skill from Baianuckles, that could master Frevo dance on that place out from his home.

The Plot:

BrazSonic and his friends are relaxing on a beach in Rio. Suddenly, BrazSonic receives an anonymous call from an alleged hostage of a kidnapper who passes his location to the hero to save him. BrazSonic believes and convinces PeloTails and Baianuckles to go with him.

After being surprised by the first boss, Christ Redeemer “with lasers”, the hostage was actually Yankow, BrazSonic’s rival, who makes a millionaire proposal to hunt some dangerous Brazilians inside. BraSonic, enchanted with the fortune offered, does not think twice and ends up accepting, even with the resistance of Baianuckles.

Throughout the adventure, the heroes would pass through the Brazilian northeast region, where they would have to deal with an old enemy of Baianuckles, that would put at risk his family; in the South, would save the Itaipu Dam, of being destroyed by a terrorist attack, and other dangers, until they discovered the true intentions of Yankow and the true enemy behind these attacks.

Levels

Rio Zone

New layout for the 2 acts, with art made from scratch, new enemies, and as a boss, the Christ Redeemer.

Cubatão Zone

The stage happens in the city of Cubatão – SP, in an industrial zone with some mangroves. It is a city famous for having spent moments of much pollution, and for its mangroves, where they inhabit crabs. In the game, Cubatão would have been taken over by robots who drove workers out of an industry, BrazSonic and his troupe would roam the place, full of traps, slick, suction tubes and mutant crabs. The layout was very similar to Sonic 2’s Chemical Plant but more apocalyptic.

Sampa Zone

The area that had most changes during those years all never had a fixed kingpin. Despite being a city full of high-speed, theoretically linear lanes, all the curves and loopings would go crazy, as well as passages where the character would be forced to flee high-speed subways and through sewers and dumps. In the 2009 version it would be totally scrapped.

Salvador Zone

Phase based in the capital of Bahia. In her Baianuckles would be behind an old enemy. The phase, of enemies, would have capoeira fighters, priests, backs, guineafowl birds that spit fire and other crazy ones. The layout of the stage itself would have influenced Sonic 3’s Carnival Night, with pinball things and underwater parts and would have the presence of one of the city’s postcards, the Lacerda Elevator.

Sertão Zone

Phase with 1 act and a roughly done layout. In the plot, Baianuckles discovers that his family has been kidnapped by his rival and some robots, and soon discovers that they are in the backlands, and leaves to rescue them without BrazSonic and PeloTails.

Chuí Zone:

Iguaçu Zone

The stage takes place at the Itaipu dam, represented as a gigantic aquatic passage, following the Sonic 3 Hydrocity Zone and Sonic 1’s Labyrinth references. There would be 3 acts in total: the first, outside the dam; the second on the inside, and the third, a little farther, in an environment of nature.

Ama Zone

Divided into 3 acts, based on Sonic 3’s Angel Island. It’s another gigantic level of the game, packed with challenges like cutting saws, waterfalls with falling wood pieces, swinging vines, wild animals and burning woods.

Acre Zone

Divided into 3 acts: the first would be a volcanic cave, which would remind Lava Reef of Sonic & Knuckles. The second, a crystal cave, with prehistoric beings. The third, an underground base full of dangers, where there would be exclusive parts to the character which is being played.

Brasília Zone

Not done

“Chefão final”

I had started a battle with the thing that was supposed to be the “final boss”. It was based on the country’s biggest TV station, but I did not go much further. In the battle, as shown in the screens below, BrazSonic in its super form, or better, Super BrazSonic can fly freely and even boost to attack the enemy.

Special Stage (versão 1)

After that pseudo-3d special stage engine, which was hard to work, I inserted a new simpler, in 2d flow. On that, the character runs through a highway in “high” speed, avoiding cars, collecting coins and eventually being chased by the police. The purpose of this Special Stage type is for you to collect a money limit before you reach the end of the stage (as shown in the progress indicator at the top of the screen). By getting the set amount of coins, the player would win one of the 7 “Mensalão” Suitcases. With 7 at hand, the character could use his “super-form” to temporarily step through the stages invincibly.

Special Stage (versão 2)

This version had begun to be used from 2008 and was more thematic than the previous ones. In it, the character would be in a soccer field. The challenge here would be to collect the number of coins that the game demanded, in order to have enough coins and blast through the goal at the end of the stage. The player could move around the sides, jump and also spin, to attack the foes. Along the way, the player would have to dodge holes, spikes, and also rival team players who attack you. At the end of the stage, having the right amount of coins in hand, the player could try to make the goal, in a kind of “Penalty”, using himself as the ball. The goalkeeper would be the main obstacle because, with each defense, the player would lose a number of coins, and if he lost all, would be detonated by a Zidane header, infamous meme of that time. If the player got the goal, he would win one of the 7 Chaos Banknotes (it had changed because of that, since Mensalão’s Suitcase scheme was already getting out of hand).

2009 Demo Build

In 2009 a functional demo of BraSonic 2 was released, with Orkut Zone, an exclusive stage that was used to show most of the game gimmicks, and with a boss battle. It was released on SAGE 2009 (Sonic Amateur Game Expo) along with other Sonic fangame productions.
