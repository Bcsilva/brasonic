BrazSonic 2 – 2005

I started the project, really, at that time, with a new engine, very close to the Sonic of Mega Drive and Gameboy Advance. With loopings, ramps, etc. etc. I even had considerable support in the forums of Portal Sonic, Sonic Fangames HQ and a few other Sonic communities, where I was able to inform the floor of the carriage of the development of the game.

Basically the game would use the same scheme of the first game, with recolored characters sprites from SEGA, with slight different props (such as the Pelotails hat and scarf and Baianuckles’ cangaceiro look), with a sequence of playable action stages slightly different from the previous one: Rio de Janeiro, São Paulo, Pelotas, Salvador, Sertão, Amazônia, and Brasília.

The Plot

BrazSonic and his friends relax on a beach in Rio. Suddenly, BrazSonic receives an anonymous call from an alleged hostage of a kidnapper who passes his location to the hero to save him. BrazSonic believes and convinces PeloTails and Baianuckles to go with him.

After being surprised by Yankow, BrazSonic’s American rival, he surrenders. Yankow decides to propose an “alliance” to hunt down corrupt politicians of the country. BrazSonic suspects at first, but with the persuasive chatter of the gringo hedgehog he ends up being persuaded to join him, to the unhappiness of Baianuckles, who nonetheless goes with them.

The “bosses” of this game are based on those politicians of the mensalão, of the PT, that involved until the ex-president Lula. In the game, these politicians would be involved in a conspiratorial plot for total domination of the country, and each region would have a different politician involved as a representative, to be defeated.

[SPOILER]: the main antagonist this time would be Rede Globo TV, as announced in the true ending of the original BrazSonic 1, as a cliffhanger, the main reason why BrazSonic 2 is a direct continuation of 1, quite obvious but good to point out.

Stages

Rio Zone

Typical tropical level like the ones from Sonic games, with some aquatic sections, linear flow, with loopings and ramps, and more open paths. In this zone, in the first act, it would happen in a beach environment, with varied enemies, from sharks to armed criminals. Act 2, on the other hand, is going on in the “favelas” (slums), with narrower paths, more gunmen and traps. An interesting detail of these stages was the fan-chars from the people of some Brazilian Sonic communities scattered in them. The idea was making them not only part of the scenario but also bring more interaction and trivia to the level exploration. The boss of this level would be Yankow, BrazSonic’s rival, armed with a pistol and grenades.

Sampa Zone

Level based on the city of São Paulo, the first act would be of the afternoon and the second one at night. The inspiration for this level was Sonic CD Stardust Speedway, and Sonic 2 Chemical Plant, both of which are urban/industrial and focused on speed. Act 2, in particular, would have a soccer stadium just before the boss, with some hooligan-like organized soccer fan group attack the player. Regarding the stage boss, it would be one of the corrupt politicians who could be attacked only in the air, gaining momentum in the explosion of their stars in the water.

Pelotas Zone

(Erroneously) Mountainous level, based in the city of Pelotas-RS and inspired by the Windy Valley of Sonic Adventure, only in 2d. There, the player would have along the way fans that would push you back, strong currents of wind, and also tornados that could persecute you.

Chuí Zone

At last a “beloved” Sonic underwater action level in BrazSonic,, based in the city of Chuí – Rio Grande do Sul, the southern place from Brazil. It is the kind of level that goes on most underwater where one of the main dangers are the moving walls that could crush you.

Sertão Zone

Based on the northeastern hinterlands, using Sonic & Knuckles’ Sandopolis and Sonic 2’s Dust Hill (stage removed from that game final version). It would be a level with plenty of spin tubes, spikes, and other things. At that time I did not do much of it.

The characters would have the same look as BrazSonic 1, using the same recycled sprites from the original Sonic games, but each with its own ability, so that the levels could be explored in different ways. PeloTails could fly temporarily (as well as Tails); Baianuckles, planar and climb walls (as Knuckles the Echidna does) and BrazSonic, bouncing on the ground to reach heights greater than the normal leap (as Sonic does in some of their games).

As for the Special Stages of the game, they would follow the same line as the original BrazSonic, but instead of following an infinite straight line, the character would go through a kind of “maze” collecting coins and dodging obstacles such as trees, rocks, and cars, all this before time runs out. At the end of the labyrinth, after surviving all the challenges, the player would conquer one of the 7 “Mensalão” Suitcases.

Technically, in order for making the Special Stages work, I used a fake 3d engine, similar to that of the old Doom games, for my lack of practice with the actual 3D Game Maker at the time. The result was cool, but it was not the most practical thing in the world for actual experience.

At that time, I used to be part of the community from Forum Sonic Portal, which was one of the largest communities of Brazilian Sonic fans of the 00s decade to promote BrazSonic 2 and its follow-up. In order to get along more in that community, I had used the same idea of the old and remarkable Sonic Time Attacked fangame. On its first level, there were several NPCs (Non-Playable Characters) scattered around the scenery, based on fan-chars (fan characters) from users of some communities of Sonic, without a role in the game’s plot, just to integrate the community and give more life to the scenarios. In this first version, with the help of some Forum Portal Sonic BrazSonic fans and supporters, who sent the animated sprites of their respective characters, I was able to add in the first stages of BrazSonic 2 successfully!

Also, I even got direct support from a member of the Fórum Portal Sonic, Rodrigo Pereira, aka Enigma Kitsune, who joined the project to compose a neat soundtrack for most of the stages.
